Invariant Limits of Complex Systems

Independent research into structural constraints on global consistency, degradation, and cyclic behavior in large-scale systems.

What this is

Enthropy Systems is an independent research effort focused on identifying invariant constraints that govern the behavior of large-scale, heterogeneous systems.

Current focus

Distribution-free certificates for heterogeneous sensor and calibration systems, the operator-algebraic rigidity that explains why they hold, and their formal (Lean 4) verification — backed by extensive simulation. Six preprints are below.

Papers

Six preprints, part of one program on structural invariants under lossy translation — from a distribution-free calibration certificate to the operator-algebraic rigidity behind it, with formal (Lean 4) proofs where stated and reproducible numerics throughout.

  • Frustration Certificates for Independently Calibrated Sensor Systems
    A distribution-free certificate for calibration inconsistency, validated on two fusion machines under one untuned pipeline.
  • Stability of Almost-Flat Lossy Cocycles: an Index Obstruction Survives Rank Loss
    Stability of almost-representations extended to non-invertible targets; the index obstruction persists through rank loss.
  • Undetectable but Unremovable: Capacity versus Topology in Lossy Networks
    A topological charge that falls below every local detection threshold yet cannot be locally removed.
  • Formalized Mathematics as a Debugging Tool
    Lean 4 formalization as an active research instrument — catching two errors in opposite directions.
  • Three Gaps, One Index
    The m-uniform rigidity of a lossy magnetic pair is topological, not spectral; a codimension theorem for the constant.
  • What a Quality Flag Does Not Encode
    A pre-registered certificate audit of a documented MAST magnetics changepoint: what archive quality flags do and don't mean.
